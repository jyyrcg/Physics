AP Physics: E & M

Work

When a force acts on an object while a displacement occurs, the force has done work on the object.

Because it is not associated with direction, work is a scalar quantity. The units of work are called a Joule (J).

A spring constant (k) measures how stiff the spring is.

Power

Power is the rate at which work is done.

Instantaneous power is the average power over a very small interval leading to a time derivative.

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy that an object possesses due to motion.

Conservative Forces

Conservation laws tell you when certain physical properties remain constant throughout the motion.

A conservative force is a force with the property that the total work done in moving a particle between two points is independent of the taken path. As a system moves under the influence of conservative forces, the individual amounts of kinetic and potential energies change, but the sum of all of them stays the same.

Potential Energy

Potential energy is energy associated with the position of the system.
