AP Physics: E & M

Impulse

When a force acts on an object over a certain time period, the force has delivered an impulse to the object. For a one-dimensional constant force, a graph of F vs. t over the interval leads to a simple interpretation of the impulse: It is the area under the curve.

Linear Momentum

Linear momentum is the force or speed of movement.

Conservation of Momentum

Conservation of momentum applies if there are no external forces to the system or if the impulse they deliver is negligible.

In the absence of external forces, the total linear momentum will stay the same.

Collisions

Collisions are a common application of the concept of one dimensions.

If the total kinetic energy is conserved, the collision is elastic.

When two objects stick together in a collision, it is an inelastic collision.
