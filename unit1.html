Kinematics

Kinematics is the study of motion without considering what is actually causing the motion.

In one dimension, there is only one axis needed: the x-axis.

The value of x is the object’s position. The change in position, the displacement, is written as delta x.

Velocity is the rate at which the position of an object changes. Average velocity is the displacement during an interval divided by the duration of the interval.

Instantaneous velocity at a given time is the average velocity over a tiny interval centered at that time.

Acceleration is the rate at which velocity changes. Just like velocity, there is also an instantaneous acceleration and an average acceleration.
